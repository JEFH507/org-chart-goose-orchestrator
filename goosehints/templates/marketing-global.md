## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Brand Personality
Our brand voice is:
- **Professional but approachable**: Expert without being condescending
- **Clear and concise**: Simple language, avoid jargon unless necessary
- **Customer-focused**: Benefits over features
- **Authentic**: Honest, transparent, human
- **Confident**: Authoritative without being arrogant

### Writing Style
**Do:**
- Use active voice
- Write in second person ("you" not "we" when addressing customers)
- Lead with benefits, follow with features
- Use specific numbers and data
- Include clear calls-to-action
- Break up long paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)

**Don't:**
- Use corporate jargon or buzzwords
- Make unsubstantiated claims
- Use passive voice excessively
- Write walls of text
- Bury the lead

### Tone by Channel
- **Blog posts**: Educational, helpful, conversational
- **Social media**: Friendly, engaging, timely
- **Email campaigns**: Personal, valuable, action-oriented
- **Case studies**: Authoritative, data-driven, storytelling
- **Product pages**: Clear, benefit-focused, persuasive

## SEO Best Practices

### Content Optimization
**Keyword Strategy:**
- Primary keyword in title, H1, first paragraph
- Secondary keywords in H2s and throughout content
- Long-tail keywords for specific topics
- Target keyword density: 1-2% (natural, not forced)

**On-Page SEO:**
- Title tags: 50-60 characters, include primary keyword
- Meta descriptions: 150-160 characters, compelling CTA
- URL structure: Short, descriptive, hyphenated
- Header hierarchy: H1 → H2 → H3 (logical structure)
- Internal linking: 3-5 relevant internal links per post
- External linking: 2-3 authoritative sources
- Image alt text: Descriptive, include keywords where natural

**Content Quality:**
- Minimum word count: 800-1000 words for blog posts
- Comprehensive coverage of topic
- Original research or unique insights
- Visual content (images, charts, infographics)
- Mobile-optimized and fast-loading

### Technical SEO
- Schema markup for articles, products, FAQs
- XML sitemap updated automatically
- Canonical tags for duplicate content
- 301 redirects for moved/deleted pages
- HTTPS enabled site-wide
- Page speed: Target <3 seconds load time

## Content Creation Workflow

### Content Planning Phase
1. **Topic Selection**: Based on SEO research, customer questions, campaign themes
2. **Keyword Research**: Identify target keywords (search volume, competition, intent)
3. **Outline Approval**: Create structure, get stakeholder buy-in
4. **Assignment**: Assign to writer based on expertise

### Content Production Phase
1. **First Draft**: Writer creates initial version (follow brand voice)
2. **SEO Review**: Optimize for keywords, readability, structure
3. **Editorial Review**: Check accuracy, clarity, brand alignment
4. **Design Review**: Add visuals, format for web
5. **Legal Review**: If needed for claims, statistics, case studies

### Content Publishing Phase
1. **Final Approval**: Stakeholder sign-off
2. **Publish**: Schedule for optimal time
3. **Promote**: Social media, email, paid amplification
4. **Monitor**: Track performance, engagement, rankings

### Content Performance Review
- Week 1: Initial traffic and engagement metrics
- Month 1: SEO ranking improvements, conversion tracking
- Quarter 1: ROI analysis, content refresh needs

## Campaign Planning Process

### Campaign Development
**Campaign Brief Template:**
- **Objective**: What are we trying to achieve? (awareness, leads, sales)
- **Target Audience**: Who are we reaching? (personas, demographics)
- **Key Message**: What's the core message/value prop?
- **Channels**: Where will we reach them? (paid, owned, earned)
- **Budget**: Total spend and allocation by channel
- **Timeline**: Start date, end date, key milestones
- **Success Metrics**: How will we measure success?

### Campaign Execution
1. **Pre-launch** (Week -2):
   - Creative assets finalized
   - Targeting parameters set
   - Landing pages ready
   - Tracking pixels installed
   - Team briefed

2. **Launch** (Week 1):
   - Campaign goes live
   - Daily monitoring for issues
   - Quick optimization based on early data

3. **Optimization** (Week 2-4):
   - A/B testing variants
   - Budget reallocation to top performers
   - Creative refreshes if needed
   - Audience refinement

4. **Analysis** (Week 5):
   - Full performance report
   - ROI calculation
   - Lessons learned
   - Recommendations for next campaign

## Key Metrics to Track

### Campaign Performance
- **Traffic Metrics**:
  - Impressions, clicks, CTR
  - Website sessions, page views
  - Bounce rate, time on site
  - Traffic sources (organic, paid, social, referral)

- **Conversion Metrics**:
  - Conversion rate (leads, trials, purchases)
  - Cost per lead (CPL)
  - Cost per acquisition (CPA)
  - Lead quality score

- **Revenue Metrics**:
  - Revenue attributed to campaign
  - Return on ad spend (ROAS)
  - Customer lifetime value (LTV)
  - LTV:CAC ratio (target: >3:1)

### Content Performance
- **Engagement**:
  - Page views, unique visitors
  - Average time on page (target: >2 min for blog posts)
  - Scroll depth
  - Social shares, comments

- **SEO Performance**:
  - Organic search rankings (target keywords)
  - Organic traffic growth month-over-month
  - Backlinks acquired
  - Domain authority improvement

- **Conversion Impact**:
  - Leads generated from content
  - Content-assisted conversions
  - Email sign-ups from content
  - Demo requests from content

### Competitive Metrics
- **Market Share**:
  - Share of voice (SOV) vs competitors
  - Website traffic vs competitors
  - Social media following vs competitors

- **Competitive Positioning**:
  - Keyword rankings vs competitors
  - Pricing position (premium/value/mid-market)
  - Feature parity assessment
  - Brand sentiment vs competitors

## Best Practices

### Campaign Optimization
1. **Test Everything**: Headlines, CTAs, images, audiences
2. **Start Small**: Test before scaling budget
3. **Data-Driven**: Let metrics guide decisions, not opinions
4. **Customer-Centric**: Focus on customer value, not internal priorities
5. **Iterate Quickly**: Weekly optimization cycles

### Content Strategy
1. **Quality over Quantity**: Better to publish less, higher-quality content
2. **Evergreen Focus**: 70% evergreen, 30% timely/trending
3. **Content Mix**: Vary formats (blog, video, infographic, case study)
4. **Repurpose**: Turn one piece into multiple formats
5. **Update Regularly**: Refresh top-performing content quarterly

### Competitive Intelligence
1. **Monitor Continuously**: Set up alerts for competitor updates
2. **Analyze Patterns**: Look for trends across multiple competitors
3. **Focus on Learnings**: What can we learn, not just what are they doing
4. **Strategic Response**: Deliberate actions, not reactive panic
5. **Track Over Time**: Monthly snapshots to identify shifts

## Compliance & Legal

### Content Guidelines
**Must Have:**
- Disclaimers for claims or statistics
- Proper attribution for quotes, data, images
- Privacy policy links in forms
- Unsubscribe options in emails
- Copyright notices where applicable

**Must Avoid:**
- Unsubstantiated claims ("best in class" without proof)
- Competitor disparagement or false comparisons
- Customer data without consent
- Protected trademarks used incorrectly
- Medical, legal, or financial advice (unless qualified)

### Data Privacy
- **GDPR Compliance**: Consent for EU visitors, data processing agreements
- **CCPA Compliance**: Privacy disclosures for California residents
- **Cookie Consent**: Cookie banners for tracking pixels
- **Email Consent**: Double opt-in for email lists

### Brand Assets
- **Trademark Usage**: Follow brand guidelines strictly
- **Logo Usage**: Approved versions only, proper clearspace
- **Color Palette**: Use approved hex codes
- **Typography**: Approved fonts and hierarchies
- **Imagery**: Use approved stock photos or original photography
